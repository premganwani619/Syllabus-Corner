Reet

Rajasthan Eligibility Examination for Teacher (REET), also known as Rajasthan Teacher Eligibility Test (RTET), is a state-level competitive exam which is conducted by the Board of Secondary Education, Rajasthan (BSER) to certify candidates for employment as teachers in the state. If you are looking to understand more about the exam, the eligibility criteria, syllabus of the exam, and like, you have come to the right place. Let us start off with the most important question, what is the REET exam?

What is REET Exam?

Basically, the REET exam is conducted for the recruitment of teachers for two different levels, namely Level I or Primary Teacher and Level II or Upper Primary Teacher. Thus, those who clear REET become eligible for the post of teachers in classes 1-5 and classes 6-8 in schools and institutions of the state. Further, as per the updated rules declared by the Rajasthan government, only Basic School Teaching Certificate (BSTC) holders are allowed to appear in the REET Level-1 examination. A lot of people ask about the REET Eligibility criteria, REET full form, and questions that resonate with the exam. The popularity of the exam is a testament to the scope of the same. The full form of REET is Rajasthan Eligibility Examination for Teachers and is one of the esteemed exams in India.

Also, with a view to giving priority to state students, the exam questions will now be related more to Rajasthan. Moreover, it has also been decided that the REET score will constitute 90% of the weightage against the current 70%, while the rest 10% would be assessed on the basis of marks scored in graduation or the scores of the Senior Secondary exam. A lot of people have doubts about whether it is difficult to crack the REET exam. With the right amount of preparation and tips, you can clear and ace the exam with ease. Ensure to understand the REET exam syllabus thoroughly and focus on the topics that you know you are comfortable with. Keep a watch out for the important REET notifications. The exam has been postponed quite a few times in 2021. Go through the REET official website and be updated.

The minimum age criteria for taking the REET exam is 18 years while there is no maximum age criteria. The exam is conducted offline and there is no negative marking.

The recruitment is done for teachers of two levels of classes: 

  1. Level I or Primary 
  2. Level II or Upper Primary.  It is important to follow a structured preparation to clear this exam and score well. Get a clear understanding of the REET syllabus, divide the topics as per the difficulty level, and allot time accordingly.
